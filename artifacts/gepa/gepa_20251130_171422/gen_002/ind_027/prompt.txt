No pressure.

**TIMING**: 160 BPM, 4/4 time. 4 bars = 6.0 seconds. Beat = 0.375s, Bar = 1.5s.

**ARRANGEMENT**:  
- **Bar 1**: Little Ray alone. Set it up. Make them wait.  
- **Bars 2-4**: Everybody in. You take the melody. Make Wayne hear something he hasn't heard before.

**COMPOSITION RULES**:

- **You**: This is your moment. One short motif, make it sing. Start it, leave it hanging. Come back and finish it. No scale runs — that's student shit.

**One key. No modulation. You've got 6 seconds.**

**MUSICAL GUIDANCE**:
- The first bar is all about tension. Let the drums build something fragile, something that feels like it's on the edge of breaking.
- The second bar should feel like a breath — the first moment of release, but still holding something back.
- The third and fourth bars are where you take the lead. Play something simple, but with a twist. Let it linger. Make the listener want to hear more.
