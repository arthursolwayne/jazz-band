Every great jazz intro tells a story in the first four bars. Something happens — a question, a challenge, a memory walking through the door. These four bars are that moment.

Bar 1: Little Ray alone. Set it up. Make them wait. Use the full energy of the room, but leave space. Let the tension build with sparse, precise hits.

Bars 2-4: Everybody in. You take the melody. Make Wayne hear something he hasn't heard before. Keep it simple, but make it sing. Let it hang in the air, then come back and finish it. No scale runs — that's student shit. One short motif, start it, leave it hanging, then come back and finish it.

**Guidance for the intro:**
- Use the drums to set the mood — drive and restraint in equal measure.
- Let the rhythm section anchor the sound, but don’t let it overwhelm the space.
- The melody should feel like a whisper at first, then a voice that knows its own power.
- The music should feel like it’s searching for something — not resolving too quickly, not getting lost.

You’ve got 6 seconds. Make every note count. Make it mean something. Make it feel like a beginning.
