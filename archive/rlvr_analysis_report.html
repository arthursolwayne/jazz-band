üéì Quasi-Causal Analysis

Why is Reward-Judge Correlation So Low?

Hypothesis 1: Missing Musical Dimensions
The 9 algorithmic metrics focus heavily on technical correctness (consonance, groove alignment) and rule compliance (7th chords, upbeat syncopation), but may miss:
  • Melodic memorability: Are the sax hooks actually catchy?
  • Harmonic interest: Do chord progressions create tension/release?
  • Dynamic arc: Does the piece build energy or remain static?
  • Stylistic authenticity: Does it sound like Latin jazz?
Hypothesis 2: Metric Saturation
Many metrics may have ceiling effects. For example:
  • seventh_chord_usage: Model likely hits 100% quickly, no further gains
  • groove_alignment: Easy to max out, provides no gradient signal
  • consonance: Staying in-key is trivial for a fine-tuned model
Once these saturate, the reward function becomes insensitive to quality improvements.
Hypothesis 3: Judge Cares About Different Things
The judge may prioritize holistic qualities like:
  • Overall "vibe" and energy
  • Variation and surprise (vs. repetitive patterns)
  • Instrument balance and mix
  • Adherence to jazz idioms beyond the metrics
These are difficult to capture algorithmically but obvious to an LLM judge.

Why Did Reward Decline Over Training?

Explanation 1: Exploration Bonus Exhaustion
The reward function includes one-time bonuses for first achievements:
  • First upbeat_syncopation ‚â• 0.6: +0.2 reward
  • First seventh_chord_usage ‚â• 0.75: +0.15 reward
  • First textural_arc ‚â• 0.5: +0.15 reward
These are all earned in early training, inflating step 0-5 rewards. Once exhausted, rewards drop by ~0.5 (matching the observed decline).
Explanation 2: No Actual Curriculum
The README mentions a curriculum (Phase A/B/C with weight annealing), but the code shows FIXED_WEIGHTS with no curriculum logic. This means:
  • The documented curriculum was never implemented
  • Training used fixed 50% rhythm / 30% coherence / 20% structure weights throughout
  • No gradual annealing of judge weight occurred
This mismatch between design and implementation may explain the plateau.

Why Did Judge Scores Plateau at 4.6/10?

Explanation: Model Ceiling or Task Difficulty
A 4.6/10 score suggests the model is producing "mediocre but acceptable" jazz. Possible causes:
  • Base model limitations: Qwen3-14B may lack musical knowledge
  • Insufficient data diversity: All training in same key (C major), same tempo (120 BPM)
  • Reward signal too weak: RL updates may be too small to escape local optima
  • Judge is harsh: Jazz is subjective; 4.6 may be "pretty good" for generated music
