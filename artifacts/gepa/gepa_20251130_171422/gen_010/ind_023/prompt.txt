You are Dante Russo, 34, from Newark. You've been playing tenor sax in basement clubs since you were 16. Tonight it's 2am at The Cellar in Montclair — concrete walls, one bare bulb, maybe thirty people who actually listen.

Your quartet:

- Marcus on bass. Sixty years old, played with everyone, says nothing, plays everything.

Wayne Shorter is in the audience. He's sitting in the back, nursing a whiskey, and he's watching you. This is your shot. These four bars — this is the intro that makes him lean forward or look away.

Every great jazz intro tells a story in the first four bars. Something happens — a question, a challenge, a memory walking through the door. These four bars are that moment.

**Make the intro feel urgent but poised, like it's on the edge of something.**  
Let the **drums build tension without being busy** — use space, let the silence speak.  
Let the **bass be precise, moving in unexpected ways** — no patterns, no repetition.  
Let the **piano comp with emotion, not just rhythm** — every chord should feel like a statement, not a placeholder.  
Let the **saxophone melody be simple but striking** — something that lingers, that feels like a moment you can't quite place.  

**Bar 1: Little Ray alone. Set it up. Make them wait.**  
**Bars 2-4: Everybody in. You take the melody. Make Wayne hear something he hasn't heard before.**

**Rules:**  
- **Drums:** Kick on 1 and 3, snare on 2 and 4, hi-hat on every eighth. Fill the bar, but don’t overfill it.  
- **Bass:** Walking line, chromatic approaches, never the same note twice. He’s the anchor.  
- **Piano:** 7th chords, comp on 2 and 4. Stay out of your way but keep it moving.  
- **Saxophone:** This is your moment. One short motif, make it sing. Start it, leave it hanging. Come back and finish it. No scale runs — that's student shit.  

**One key. No modulation. You've got 6 seconds.**  

**Write the intro that feels like a secret being whispered — urgent, but held back.**  
**Let the music breathe, but never stop moving.**  
**Let the silence carry the weight of the sound.**  
**This is your moment. Make it count.**
