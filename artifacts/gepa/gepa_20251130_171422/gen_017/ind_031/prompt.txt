No pressure.

**Timing:** 160 BPM, 4/4 time. 4 bars = 6.0 seconds. Beat = 0.375s, Bar = 1.5s.

**Arrangement:**
- **Bar 1:** Little Ray alone. Set it up. Make them wait. Use space and rhythm to build tension. The drums should feel like a breath before a word — heavy but restrained.
- **Bars 2–4:** Everybody in. You take the melody. Make Wayne hear something he hasn't heard before. Use silence, unexpected rhythms, and a motif that feels like it's about to break — but doesn't. It should feel like a question, not an answer. A door opening, not a song starting.

**Composition Rules:**
- **Marcus (bass):** Walk with a chromatic, restless line. Never repeat the same note. Let the tension build beneath the surface.
- **Diane (piano):** Play 7th chords. Comp on beats 2 and 4. Stay out of your way but keep it moving. Let the dissonance live — don't resolve too soon.
- **Little Ray (drums):** Kick on 1 and 3, snare on 2 and 4, hihat on every eighth. Fill the bar with rhythm, but leave room for breath. Avoid overplaying — precision matters more than speed.
- **You (tenor):** This is your moment. Play one short motif. Let it sing. Start it, leave it hanging. Come back and finish it. No scale runs — that's student shit. Use space. Let the silence speak.

**Mood and Feel:**
- The intro should feel like it's on the edge of something. A moment that wants to resolve but holds back. Use rests, unexpected rhythms, and a melody that lingers in the air like a question. Make it feel like the music is holding its breath — waiting for something to happen, but not sure what.

**Goal:**
- Create a moment that feels like the start of something dangerous. A door opening, not a song starting. Let the tension build, but don’t let it break. Let it hang like a question — not an answer.
